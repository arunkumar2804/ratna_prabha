K. Ratna Prabha

K. Ratna Prabha (born March 13, 1958) is a retired Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer of the 1981 batch who served as the 37th Chief Secretary of Karnataka. She made history as the third woman to lead the state's bureaucracy and the first Dalit woman to hold the prestigious position[1].

Known for her proactive administration and "rockstar" public image, she has been a vocal advocate for women's entrepreneurship and digital governance. Following her retirement, she transitioned into politics, joining the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and contesting parliamentary elections[3].

Early Life and Education

Born in Ongole, Andhra Pradesh, Ratna Prabha comes from a distinguished family of civil servants and pioneers. Her father, K. Chandraiah, was a senior IAS officer, and her mother was notably one of the first women doctors from the coastal town of Karkala[16].

She completed her primary and secondary education in Hyderabad. She holds a Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) and further pursued postgraduate studies in Sociology and English, providing her with a versatile academic background that would later inform her social-centric approach to administration[17].

Professional IAS Career

Ratna Prabha's career in the IAS spanned nearly four decades, characterized by significant firsts and transformative leadership:

  • Bidar Period (1983): She was appointed as the first woman Assistant Commissioner of Bidar, where she gained popularity for her accessibility and grassroots development work.

  • Chikmagalur (1986): Served as Special Deputy Commissioner and Project Director, focusing on rural infrastructure and healthcare.

  • Commerce & Industries: As Additional Chief Secretary, she spearheaded the "Invest Karnataka" initiative and was instrumental in positioning Bengaluru as a global startup hub[14].

Tenure as Chief Secretary

In November 2017, she was appointed as the Chief Secretary of Karnataka. Her tenure was marked by a unique administrative synergy, as she worked alongside Neelamani N. Raju, the state's first woman DG & IGP, marking the first time both top administrative and policing roles in an Indian state were held by women simultaneously[6].

Her leadership was noted for its focus on digitization, transparency in government tenders, and the empowerment of women entrepreneurs through state policy support.

Political Transition

Following her retirement in June 2018, Ratna Prabha entered the political arena. She officially joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in April 2019, citing a desire to continue public service through policy and governance from a political standpoint[15].

In 2021, the BJP nominated her as the candidate for the Tirupati Lok Sabha by-election, recognizing her administrative acumen and her roots in the region[12].

Awards and Recognition

Tun Dr. Mahathir Mohamad Good Global Exemplary Leadership Award (2025) Recognized in Malaysia for her global leadership and unwavering commitment to the economic empowerment of women.
Public Service Excellence Award Awarded for her transformative work in the Bidar district during her early career.

Entrepreneurship & Ubuntu

Post-retirement, she founded the Ubuntu Consortium of Women Entrepreneurs Associations. Under her leadership as President, Ubuntu has grown into a powerful platform connecting thousands of women entrepreneurs across India, providing them with mentorship, market access, and financial handholding[20].
